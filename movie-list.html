Grease is a 1978 musical film set in the 1950s and 1960s, directed by Randal Kleiser and starring John Travolta and Olivia Newton-John. It is based on the 1972 musical of the same name created by Jim Jacobs and Warren Casey. The film was a resounding success and cemented the careers of several artists (Travolta and Newton-John, Stockard Channing, Jeff Conaway, and some supporting characters like actor Lorenzo Lamas). The musical tells the love story of rebel Danny Zuko (John Travolta) and innocent Sandy Olsson (Olivia Newton-John). They both meet during the summer and at the time of their separation, neither believed they would see each other again. But they were wrong: when she decides to enroll in the same school as her friend Frenchy (Didi Conn), the last thing she expected was to become Danny's classmate, co-leader of the T-Birds rebel gang.

The Wizard of Oz is an American musical-fantasy film released in 1939 based on the story "The Wonderful Wizard of Oz" written by L. Frank Baum. The main director was Victor Fleming, who had to leave the set to take over the film project "Gone with the Wind," and the leading roles in the film were played by Judy Garland, Ray Bolger, Jack Haley, and Bert Lahr. The film tells the story of schoolgirl Dorothy Gale (Judy Garland), who lives on a farm in Kansas with Aunt Em and Uncle Henry, and dreams of a better place "somewhere over the rainbow." After being taken by a tornado and losing consciousness, Dorothy dreams that she, her dog Toto, and her house are flying to the magical land of Oz. There, the Good Witch of the North, Glinda (Billie Burk), advises Dorothy to follow the yellow brick road to the Emerald City, where she will meet the Wizard of Oz, the only one who can send her home to Kansas. During her journey, she meets the Scarecrow, the Tin Man, and the Cowardly Lion, who accompany her, hoping to gain what they lack: a brain, a heart, and courage. Meanwhile, the characters try to avoid the Wicked Witch of the West (Margaret Hamilton), who wants to take the ruby slippers received by Dorothy from Glinda.

The Chronicles of Narnia - The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe is an American and British film from 2005, directed by Andrew Adamson. The leading roles were played by actors William Moseley, Anna Popplewell, Skandar Keynes, Georgie Henley, Liam Neeson, Tilda Swinton, James McAvoy, Jim Broadbent, Ray Winstone and Dawn French. The film is based on the epic fantasy novel for children The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe by Clive Staples Lewis. The novel is the first published in The Chronicles of Narnia series and the second in chronological order.

Marty McFly is a typical American teenager of the 80s. He lives with his two siblings and parents in Hill Valley, an imaginary town in California. He has a beautiful girlfriend, Jennifer Parker. One night in October 1985, he is accidentally sent back in time to November 5, 1955, in a time-traveling DeLorean car fueled by plutonium, whose inventor "Doc" Emmett Brown is a crazy scientist. During the incredible time-traveling journey, Marty must make sure that his parents, who are teenagers now, played by Crispin Glover and Lea Thompson, meet and fall in love so that he can return to the future.

Together with her best friend Dionne, she leads a luxurious life, often teased by her former stepbrother, the idealistic Josh, for being superficial and selfish. Determined to do good deeds and prove Josh wrong about her, Cher takes on the role of matchmaker and manages to marry two lonely teachers from her high school - Mr. Hall and Mrs. Geist - and takes Tai Frasier, a new girl at their school who doesn't know the secrets of popularity, under her protective wing, trying to set her up with a wealthy and snobbish boy named Elton, even though Tai is in love with Travis.
